Achievements
IV Solution
2025 ongoing  /  Rigshospitalet x Copenhagen Health Innovation
Redesigning mobility for patients on IV drops
As part of the 3rd semester in Sustainable Design at Aalborg University, my project and I collaborated with Rigshospitalet and Copenhagen Health Innovation (CHI) to develop a more flexible and user-friendly solution for patients connected to IV drip stands and infusion pumps. The goal was to increase patient mobility during hospital stays, an area I care deeply about through my broader personal experience within the healthcare system, and one that has a direct impact on recovery and quality of life. The project culminated in a pitch event, where we received feedback and perspectives from clinicians and hospital staff, including the team at Ward 5054.

Work

My interest in health technology is deeply personal. Growing up, hospitals were never far away. Allergies, asthma and severe reactions meant that healthcare systems were not something abstract to me, they were something I experienced from the inside. At one point, a hospital error led to me being given something I'm severely allergic to. I went into anaphylactic shock and ended up on a respirator. That experience fundamentally changed how I think about healthcare systems and responsibility. Afterwards, one thought kept coming back to me: "How the hell do I make sure this doesn't happen to someone else?" That question stayed with me. It made me realise how much the design of systems, processes and communication matters in environments where people depend on them working properly. Since then I've been interested in understanding healthcare both from the patient side and from a systems perspective.

IV drip mobility solution and patient-centred design
LCA on Cytoset and Life Cycle Assessment
Patient safeness and anaphylaxis and system errors
Perspectives
Perspective

Healthcare is a field filled with incredibly skilled and dedicated professionals. At the same time, when you look at the system from the outside, you realise how much everyday work is shaped by routines, habits and complex workflows. New technologies and solutions often fail not because they are technically poor, but because they are difficult to integrate into the everyday reality of healthcare professionals. For me, the most interesting questions sit exactly there.

EasyGreen via Medley VC

A Medley.VC journey
EasyGreen was born out of Medley, a Danish venture capital, whose model is to build and scale companies from within. Being trusted as one of the first people in meant carrying real responsibility, from day one. I worked across business development, sales, recruitment, platform design, and internal processes, and played a role in generating roughly 22% of total revenue leading up to the acquisition by Modstrom in 2023. Working this closely with a VC-backed venture gave me a front-row seat to how startups are built, structured, and eventually scaled. Medley's approach: building companies with deliberate intention, strong founders, and a clear thesis, shaped how I think about what it takes to go from zero to something real.

VC Explorer Programme
Over two weekends in Aarhus, I took part in the VC Explorer Programme, gaining insight into how venture capital works in practice. From understanding deal flow and risk assessment to thinking through investment cases and VC founder dynamics, the programme provided a structured look into how ventures are identified and developed.
